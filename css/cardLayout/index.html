Card Anatomy

Each card design has 4 main parts.

  • card: full, left, right, small, medium, large
  • header: left, center, right
  • content: left, center, right
  • *footer: left, center, right

Cards can be made to span the entire width of the screen. They can also encompass only the left side, or only the right side. A card, by default, will size itself vertically based on it's containing content. However, there are a few options for fixed sizes which make matching a left and right card easier. Be aware that fixed size cards do not support footers at this time. If you intend to use a footer, use either a full size card, or a half size card without a fixed size.

Headers come in 3 positions. Left, centered, and right. You can only use 1 header per card. Headers are supported on all variations of the card

Content is where you will put the bulk of a card. By default, it will be left justified, but you can specify centered or right justification by adding the appropriate class.

Footers are used in the same way as headers. Left, center, and right positions are supported on all cards except fixed size cards. If you intend to use a footer, you must choose a dynamically sizing card type.

Each card should use only what it needs, and nothing extra. The following cards will show you the different options you can use to build your cards. Header and footer placements can be mixed and match. For instance, you can combine a left header and a right footer, or a center header and a left footer. Use whatever combinations you feel are best for your content. Be aware that using multiple headers or multiple footers on a single card does not work.
